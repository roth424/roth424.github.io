Is there a connection to weather changes the closer you are to the equator?

The purpose of this project is to show cities weather including temperature, humidity, cloudiness, and wind speed at the different latitude points. Latitude is a geographic coordinate specifying the location point of a place north or south of the equator. It is expressed by angular measurement ranging from 0 degrees at the equator and 90 degrees at the poles. Different latitudes receive different amounts of sunlight. The higher the latitudes are, the less sunlight those locations receive, so those locations receive less heat. Latitudes near equator are warmer than those further away either north or south.


What is Latitude?

Latitude is a geographic coordinate that specifies the north south position of a point on the Earths surface. Latitude is an angle, usually expressed in degrees and minutes, which ranges from 0 degrees at the Equator to 90 degrees at the poles. Lines of constant latitude, run east west as circles parallel to the equator.
